Women’s Singles Champion

Consistency and courage lead to greatness—Sabalenka proves it with every match.

Aryna Sabalenka successfully defended her U.S. Open women's singles title by defeating American Amanda Anisimova 6–3, 7–6(7–3) in the final held on September 6, 2025, at Arthur Ashe Stadium in New York. This victory marked her fourth Grand Slam singles title, following her wins at the Australian Open in 2023 and 2024, and the U.S. Open in 2024. With this achievement, Sabalenka became the first woman since Serena Williams in 2014 to win back-to-back U.S. Open titles Reuters . Sabalenka, currently ranked world No. 1, showcased remarkable consistency and mental resilience throughout the match. She committed only 15 unforced errors compared to Anisimova's 29, demonstrating her dominance on hard courts Reuters . The final was a thrilling contest, with Anisimova, the 24-year-old American, putting up a strong fight. Despite leading by a break in the second set and serving for the match at 5–4, Sabalenka faced challenges. However, she regained composure and clinched the victory in a tiebreak, securing her place in tennis history CNBC . This triumph underscores Sabalenka's status as one of the premier players in women's tennis, solidifying her legacy with a second consecutive U.S. Open title.
